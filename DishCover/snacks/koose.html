Koose

About This Recipe

Koose (also known as 'Akara' in some regions) is a delicious Ghanaian fritter made from blended black-eyed beans, seasoned with onions, pepper, and spices, then deep-fried until golden brown and crispy. It's a popular street food and can be enjoyed with porridge or as a side dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried black-eyed beans
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1-2 fresh chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour (optional, for binding)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

  1. Soak the dried black-eyed beans in water overnight or for at least 4-6 hours to soften.
  2. Drain and peel the beans by rubbing them between your hands or using a food processor to remove the skins.
  3. Blend the peeled beans with onions, fresh chilies, ginger (if using), and a little water until you get a smooth, thick batter.
  4. Transfer the batter to a bowl and stir in the ground pepper and salt to taste. If the batter is too runny, add a tablespoon of flour to help bind it.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat.
  6. Using a spoon or your hands, drop spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil. Fry in batches, turning the Koose until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  7. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  8. Serve the Koose hot, either with porridge, as a snack, or with a side of spicy pepper sauce.
