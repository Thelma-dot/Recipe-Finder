Akara

About This Recipe

Akara, also known as bean cakes or bean fritters, is a deep-fried snack made from blended black-eyed peas. It is a popular dish in Ghana and other West African countries, often served with pepper sauce or porridge.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups black-eyed peas
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for spice)
  • 1 small bell pepper (chopped, optional)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. Soak the black-eyed peas in water for at least 3 hours or overnight.
  2. Rinse and rub the peas between your hands to remove the outer skins, then drain.
  3. Blend the peeled peas with onion, bell pepper, ginger, black pepper, cayenne pepper, salt, and water until smooth.
  4. Transfer the mixture to a bowl and stir well to aerate it.
  5. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  6. Drop spoonfuls of the mixture into the hot oil and fry until golden brown, turning occasionally.
  7. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve warm with spicy pepper sauce or as a side dish.
