Mpotompoto

About This Recipe

Mpotompoto is a Ghanaian comfort food made from boiled yam that is mashed and served as a side dish with soup or stew. It's soft, creamy, and pairs well with a variety of stews or light soups, often enjoyed with meats or fish.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium-sized yams (preferably white yam)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or margarine
  • 1/2 cup milk or as needed
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground pepper (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for cooking)

Instructions

  1. Peel and cut the yams into medium-sized chunks.
  2. Boil the yam pieces in a large pot with enough water to cover them. Add a pinch of salt to the water and cook for about 20-30 minutes, or until the yams are soft and easily pierced with a fork.
  3. Once the yams are cooked, drain the water and return the yams to the pot.
  4. Use a potato masher or a fork to mash the yams until smooth and creamy. If the mixture is too thick, add milk or water gradually until you reach the desired consistency.
  5. Stir in the butter or margarine, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Continue mashing until the Mpotompoto is smooth and fluffy.
  7. Serve warm as a side dish with your favorite Ghanaian soup or stew, such as light soup, groundnut soup, or palm nut soup.
