Kulikuli

About This Recipe

Kulikuli is a crunchy and flavorful Ghanaian snack made from groundnuts (peanuts), seasoned with spices and deep-fried to perfection. It’s a popular snack enjoyed as a quick bite, often served with porridge or enjoyed on its own.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups groundnuts (peanuts), shelled and roasted
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for spice)
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Water (for binding the dough)

Instructions

  1. Roast the groundnuts (peanuts) in a dry pan over medium heat until they are golden brown and fragrant. Allow them to cool completely.
  2. Once cooled, grind the roasted groundnuts into a fine paste using a food processor or mortar and pestle.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the groundnut paste with grated ginger, minced garlic, cayenne pepper, ground pepper, and salt. Mix thoroughly.
  4. Gradually add a small amount of water to the mixture and knead it into a dough-like consistency. The dough should be firm and not too sticky.
  5. Take small portions of the dough and roll them into small balls or log shapes, depending on your preference.
  6. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium heat for frying.
  7. Carefully drop the dough shapes into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-5 minutes per batch.
  8. Remove the fried kulikuli from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  9. Allow to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy your kulikuli as a snack or with porridge!
