Zowey

About This Recipe

Zowey is a classic Ghanaian dish made from cornmeal, often served with soup or stew. It has a smooth, creamy texture and is often enjoyed as a staple dish, especially for breakfast or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cornmeal
  • 4 cups water (or as needed)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, for sweetness)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon butter or margarine (optional, for richness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional, for flavor)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the cornmeal with a small amount of water to form a smooth paste.
  3. Once the water is boiling, slowly pour the cornmeal paste into the pot while stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.
  4. Reduce the heat to medium-low and continue stirring the mixture to ensure it doesn't stick to the bottom of the pot.
  5. Cook the cornmeal mixture for about 10-15 minutes, until it thickens and becomes smooth.
  6. Season with salt, sugar (if using), and butter (if using). Stir in the vanilla extract for added flavor, if desired.
  7. Continue to cook for another 5-10 minutes until the consistency reaches a creamy, pudding-like texture.
  8. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly before serving. Zowey can be served with your favorite soup or stew for a complete meal.
