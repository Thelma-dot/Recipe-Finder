Atwemo

About This Recipe

Atwemo, also known as Ghanaian twisted doughnuts, is a crunchy and slightly sweet snack made from deep-fried dough. It is commonly enjoyed as a street snack or during festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 2 tablespoons margarine or butter
  • 1/2 cup evaporated milk (or water)
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • Oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, salt, baking powder, and nutmeg.
  2. Rub in the butter or margarine until the mixture becomes crumbly.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until a smooth dough forms.
  5. Cover and let the dough rest for about 15 minutes.
  6. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut into thin strips.
  7. Twist each strip into a rope-like shape or any desired design.
  8. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  9. Fry the twisted dough in batches until golden brown, turning occasionally.
  10. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  11. Allow to cool before serving.
  12. Enjoy as a crispy snack with tea or a cold drink!
