Kosua ne Meko

About This Recipe

Kosua ne Meko is a simple but delicious Ghanaian dish made from hard-boiled eggs served with a spicy, savory tomato and pepper sauce. It’s a common breakfast or light meal, perfect for pairing with rice or plantains.

Ingredients

  • 4-6 large eggs
  • 2 medium-sized tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1-2 fresh chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste (optional, for richer color)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper (optional)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon curry powder (optional, for flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme (optional, for aroma)

Instructions

  1. Hard-boil the eggs by placing them in a pot of water and boiling for about 10-12 minutes. Once cooked, peel and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Add the chopped onions to the pan and sauté for 2-3 minutes, or until they are soft and translucent.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and fresh chilies to the pan and cook, stirring occasionally, for about 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes break down and form a sauce.
  5. If desired, add tomato paste, ground pepper, curry powder, and dried thyme, and cook for another 2-3 minutes to enhance the flavors.
  6. Season the sauce with salt to taste, and let it simmer for a couple of minutes to thicken.
  7. Cut the hard-boiled eggs into halves or quarters and gently add them to the sauce. Stir carefully to coat the eggs in the sauce.
  8. Let the eggs simmer in the sauce for an additional 3-5 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  9. Serve the Kosua ne Meko with rice, fried plantains, or any starch of your choice.
