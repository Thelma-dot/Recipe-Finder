Kelewele

About This Recipe

Kelewele is a flavorful Ghanaian street food made from ripe plantains, seasoned with spices like ginger, garlic, and cayenne pepper. It's a popular snack or side dish that is both sweet and spicy, perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe plantains
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 1 tablespoon paprika (optional, for color)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Salt, to taste
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. Peel the plantains and cut them into bite-sized chunks or diagonal slices.
  2. In a bowl, mix the ginger, garlic, cayenne pepper, ground pepper, paprika, sugar, and salt.
  3. Add the plantain pieces to the bowl and toss to coat them evenly with the spice mixture. Let it marinate for about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or frying pot over medium-high heat.
  5. Carefully add the plantains to the hot oil in batches, frying them until golden brown and crispy, about 4-5 minutes per batch.
  6. Remove the fried Kelewele from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. Serve immediately as a snack or side dish, with a cold drink or as part of a meal.
