Bofrot

About This Recipe

Bofrot, also known as Puff-Puff in some parts of West Africa, is a deep-fried dough snack that is soft on the inside and crispy on the outside. It is a popular street food in Ghana, often enjoyed with tea or soft drinks.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm water or milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Instructions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, salt, nutmeg, and yeast.
  2. Gradually add warm water or milk while stirring to form a thick, smooth batter.
  3. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let the batter rise for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  5. Using a spoon or your hands, scoop small portions of the batter and drop them into the hot oil.
  6. Fry until golden brown, turning occasionally for even cooking.
  7. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  8. Let them cool slightly before serving.
  9. Enjoy your Bofrot warm as a snack with tea or a soft drink!
