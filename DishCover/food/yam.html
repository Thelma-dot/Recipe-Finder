Boiled Yam & Kontomire stew

About This Recipe

Boiled yam with Kontomire (cocoyam leaves) stew is a popular Ghanaian dish made with tender boiled yam and a rich, flavorful spinach-like stew cooked with palm oil, onions, tomatoes, and smoked fish.

Ingredients

For the Boiled Yam:

  • 1 medium-sized yam
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Water (for boiling)

For the Kontomire Stew:

  • 2 cups kontomire (cocoyam leaves) or spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 2 fresh tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper (optional)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 smoked fish or salted fish (deboned and flaked)
  • 1/2 cup groundnut paste (optional, for a richer taste)
  • 1 teaspoon shrimp powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

Boiling the Yam:

  1. Peel and cut the yam into large chunks.
  2. Rinse and place in a pot, adding enough water to cover the yam.
  3. Add salt and boil for 15-20 minutes until tender.
  4. Drain the water and set the yam aside.

Preparing the Kontomire Stew:

  1. Heat palm oil in a saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and sauté until soft.
  3. Stir in garlic, ginger, and tomato paste, cooking for 2 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add the smoked or salted fish, stirring well.
  6. Stir in the groundnut paste (if using) and shrimp powder.
  7. Add the chopped kontomire leaves and stir until wilted.
  8. Let the stew simmer for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  9. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

Serving:

  • Serve the boiled yam hot with the Kontomire stew.
  • Enjoy with a side of boiled eggs or avocado for extra flavor.
