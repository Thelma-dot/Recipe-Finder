Kokonte & Groundnut Soup

About This Recipe

Kokonte, often referred to as "face of the earth," is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from dried cassava flour. It is commonly served with groundnut soup, which is a rich and flavorful peanut-based soup. Kokonte and groundnut soup are often enjoyed together as a hearty and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of dried cassava flour (Kokonte flour)
  • 4 cups of water (for the Kokonte)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (for the Kokonte)
  • 1 pound chicken, beef, or goat meat (for the soup)
  • 1 cup groundnut (peanuts), roasted and blended into paste
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1-2 teaspoons ground pepper (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon ground garlic (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 liter of water (for the soup)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 bouillon cube (optional, for flavor)

Instructions

  1. To prepare the Kokonte, bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a pot. Add a pinch of salt to the water.
  2. Gradually add the dried cassava flour (Kokonte flour) to the boiling water, stirring constantly to avoid lumps. Continue to stir until the mixture thickens and the Kokonte becomes smooth and firm. Let it cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Once cooked, set aside.
  3. For the groundnut soup, heat the palm oil or vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they are soft and translucent.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, ground pepper, ginger, and garlic to the pot. Stir and cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes soften and the mixture becomes fragrant.
  5. Add the blended groundnut paste (peanut paste) to the pot and stir to combine. Cook for another 5 minutes to allow the peanuts to release their flavors.
  6. Add the meat (chicken, beef, or goat meat) to the pot and pour in 1 liter of water. Stir well, ensuring the meat is fully submerged. If you are using a bouillon cube, add it to the pot now for extra flavor.
  7. Cover the pot and simmer the soup for about 30-45 minutes, or until the meat is tender and the soup has thickened. Stir occasionally and check the seasoning, adding salt as needed.
  8. Once the soup is ready and the flavors are well combined, serve it hot alongside the Kokonte.
  9. To serve, place a portion of Kokonte on a plate and ladle the groundnut soup over it. Enjoy your meal!
