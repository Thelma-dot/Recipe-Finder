Fufu & Palmnut Soup

About This Recipe

Fufu and Groundnut Soup is a rich and flavorful Ghanaian dish. Groundnut (peanut) soup is made with a creamy peanut butter base, tomatoes, and spices, often served with meat or fish alongside soft, stretchy fufu.

Ingredients for Fufu

  • 2 medium-sized cassavas
  • 2 ripe plantains
  • Water (for boiling)
  • Fufu powder (alternative for instant fufu)

Ingredients for Groundnut Soup

  • 1 cup unsweetened peanut butter
  • 1 pound (500g) chicken, beef, goat meat, or fish
  • 3 fresh tomatoes
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for extra spice)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 4 cups water or stock
  • 1 seasoning cube (optional)
  • 1 small piece of prekese (optional, for extra flavor)

Instructions for Fufu

  1. Peel the cassava and plantains, then cut them into small pieces.
  2. Boil them in a pot of water until soft.
  3. Drain the water and pound them together using a mortar and pestle until smooth and stretchy.
  4. Alternatively, blend the boiled cassava and plantain, then stir over low heat until smooth.
  5. Shape the fufu into small balls and set aside.

Instructions for Groundnut Soup

  1. In a pot, season the meat or fish with salt, black pepper, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Add chopped onions and a little water, then steam for about 10 minutes.
  3. Blend the fresh tomatoes and add them to the pot along with tomato paste.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix peanut butter with 1 cup of warm water until smooth.
  5. Pour the peanut butter mixture into the pot, followed by the remaining water or stock.
  6. Add cayenne pepper, seasoning cube, and prekese if using.
  7. Allow the soup to simmer for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Adjust seasoning as needed and let it cook until the oil rises to the top.
  9. Remove from heat and serve hot with fufu.
