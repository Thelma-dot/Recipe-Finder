Kelewele

About This Recipe

Angwamo, also known as Ghanaian Oil Rice, is a simple but flavorful dish made by frying rice in oil before cooking it. It is often served with fried eggs, sardines, or avocado.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice (preferably long-grain)
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil or palm oil
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (optional)
  • 1 stock cube (optional, for extra flavor)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  3. Stir in the garlic and ginger (if using), then add the rice.
  4. Fry the rice for about 3-5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add water and salt, then bring to a boil.
  6. Cover and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked.
  7. Fluff the rice and serve with fried eggs, sardines, or avocado.
