YakaYake

About This Recipe

YakaYake is a delightful, savory snack originating from Ghana, made from ground corn, spices, and other ingredients. It’s commonly fried until crispy and enjoyed as a delicious street food or appetizer.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cornmeal (or ground corn)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground pepper (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup warm water (for mixing)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the cornmeal, onion, ground pepper, ginger, turmeric, and salt.
  2. Slowly add the warm water to the mixture, stirring until a thick dough forms.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls or patties, depending on your preferred size.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium heat for frying.
  5. Carefully add the dough balls or patties to the hot oil in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  6. Fry the YakaYake until golden brown and crispy, about 4-5 minutes per side.
  7. Remove the YakaYake from the oil and drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  8. Allow the YakaYake to cool slightly before serving as a delicious snack or appetizer.
