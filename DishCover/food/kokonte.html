Kokonte with Palmnut soup

About This Recipe

Kokonte, also known as "face the wall," is a Ghanaian dish made from dried cassava flour. It is commonly enjoyed with Palm Nut Soup, a rich and hearty soup made from palm fruit extract, meat, and spices.

Ingredients

For Kokonte:

  • 2 cups dried cassava flour
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (optional)

For Palm Nut Soup:

  • 4 cups palm fruit concentrate (or freshly processed palm nut extract)
  • 1 pound assorted meat (goat, beef, or chicken)
  • 1/2 pound smoked fish (flaked)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 fresh tomatoes (blended)
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon shrimp seasoning (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 4 cups meat broth or water

Instructions

Preparing Kokonte:

  1. Boil 3 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Mix 1 cup of water with the cassava flour in a bowl to form a smooth paste.
  3. Gradually add the paste to the boiling water while stirring continuously.
  4. Reduce heat and keep stirring until the mixture thickens into a smooth, stretchy consistency.
  5. Cover and let it steam for 3–5 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with Palm Nut Soup.

Preparing Palm Nut Soup:

  1. In a pot, add meat, chopped onions, garlic, ginger, salt, and shrimp seasoning. Let it steam for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the blended tomatoes, ground crayfish, and pepper. Stir and cook for another 10 minutes.
  3. Pour in the palm nut concentrate and meat broth. Stir well.
  4. Let the soup simmer for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add smoked fish and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Adjust seasoning and serve hot with Kokonte.
