Egusi Soup with Riceballs

About This Recipe

Egusi Soup is a flavorful West African dish made with melon seeds, vegetables, and assorted meats. It is often paired with rice balls (Omo Tuo) in Ghana, creating a rich and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

For Egusi Soup:

  • 2 cups egusi (melon seeds), ground
  • 1 pound meat (goat, beef, or chicken)
  • 1/2 pound smoked fish (flaked)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 fresh tomatoes (blended)
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (minced)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon shrimp seasoning (optional)
  • 2 cups spinach or kontomire (cocoyam leaves), chopped
  • 2 cups meat broth or water

For Rice Balls (Omo Tuo):

  • 2 cups rice (preferably short-grain or broken rice)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (optional)

Instructions

Preparing Egusi Soup:

  1. In a pot, heat palm oil over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add blended tomatoes, pepper, and crayfish. Cook for 10 minutes until thick.
  4. Mix ground egusi with a little water to form a thick paste, then add to the pot.
  5. Stir and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Add meat, smoked fish, and broth. Stir and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  7. Add chopped spinach or kontomire and cook for another 5 minutes.
  8. Adjust salt and seasoning to taste.

Preparing Rice Balls (Omo Tuo):

  1. Wash the rice and add it to a pot with 4 cups of water.
  2. Cook on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice becomes very soft.
  3. Once fully cooked, mash the rice with a wooden spoon to form a sticky consistency.
  4. Wet your hands with water and shape the mashed rice into smooth balls.
  5. Serve hot with Egusi Soup.
