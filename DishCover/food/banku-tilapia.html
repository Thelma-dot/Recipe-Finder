Banku and Tilapia

About This Recipe

Banku and Tilapia is a classic Ghanaian dish, popular in the Greater Accra region and beyond. Banku is made from fermented corn and cassava dough, while grilled tilapia is seasoned with flavorful spices. It is often served with hot pepper sauce (shito) and fresh vegetables.

Ingredients

For the Banku:

  • 2 cups corn dough
  • 1 cup cassava dough
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2-3 cups water (as needed)

For the Grilled Tilapia:

  • 1 whole tilapia (cleaned and gutted)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for spice)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 lemon (sliced for garnish)

For the Pepper Sauce:

  • 4 fresh tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 red chilies (adjust to spice preference)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

Instructions

Preparing the Banku:

  1. In a pot, mix the corn dough and cassava dough with water to form a smooth, thick paste.
  2. Add salt and place the pot on medium heat.
  3. Stir continuously with a wooden spatula to prevent lumps.
  4. Gradually add more water if needed and keep stirring until the mixture thickens.
  5. Cook for about 15-20 minutes until it forms a smooth, elastic dough.
  6. Shape into balls and set aside.

Grilling the Tilapia:

  1. Make small cuts on the fish to allow the seasoning to penetrate.
  2. Mix garlic, ginger, paprika, black pepper, salt, cayenne pepper, and oil to form a marinade.
  3. Rub the marinade all over the fish, ensuring it gets into the cuts.
  4. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes for better flavor.
  5. Grill on a charcoal grill or oven at 180°C (350°F) for about 20-30 minutes, flipping halfway.
  6. Once golden brown and cooked through, remove and garnish with lemon slices.

Preparing the Pepper Sauce:

  1. Blend the tomatoes, chilies, and onions into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and fry the mixture for about 10 minutes.
  3. Add salt to taste and remove from heat.

Serving:

  • Serve the grilled tilapia with banku and pepper sauce.
  • Garnish with fresh vegetables like onions, tomatoes, and bell peppers.
  • Enjoy with a cold drink!
