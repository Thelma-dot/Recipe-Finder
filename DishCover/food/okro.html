Banku And Okro Stew

About This Recipe

Banku and Okro Stew is a traditional Ghanaian dish. Banku is made from fermented corn and cassava dough, cooked into a smooth, slightly sour dough. It pairs perfectly with Okro Stew, which is rich in vegetables, seafood, or meat, with a unique slimy texture that enhances the flavor.

Banku Ingredients

  • 2 cups corn dough
  • 1 cup cassava dough
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 3 cups water (adjust as needed)

Okro Stew Ingredients

Vegetables & Base:

  • 2 cups fresh okro (chopped or blended)
  • 2 fresh tomatoes (blended or chopped)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 cup spinach (or kontomire leaves)

Protein (Choose Your Favorite):

  • 1 cup smoked fish (e.g., salmon or tilapia)
  • 1 cup beef or goat meat (cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup fresh shrimp or crabs
  • 1/2 cup dried herrings (optional)

Spices & Seasoning:

  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for spice)
  • 1 teaspoon shrimp powder (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dawa dawa (fermented locust beans, optional)
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil or vegetable oil

How to Make Banku

  1. In a large pot, mix the corn dough and cassava dough with 2 cups of water until smooth.
  2. Place the pot over medium heat and stir continuously using a wooden spatula.
  3. As the mixture thickens, add the remaining water gradually, stirring to prevent lumps.
  4. Continue stirring and kneading for about 15-20 minutes until it forms a smooth, stretchy dough.
  5. Add salt and mix well.
  6. Once fully cooked and smooth, mold into round shapes using a small bowl or spoon.
  7. Serve hot with Okro Stew.

How to Make Okro Stew

  1. Boil chopped okro with a little water for 5 minutes until slimy. Set aside.
  2. In a pot, heat palm oil and sauté the onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  3. Add the meat, shrimp, or fish, season with salt, black pepper, and shrimp powder, then cook until tender.
  4. Stir in blended tomatoes and cook for about 10 minutes until thickened.
  5. Add the cooked okro, spinach, and any additional seafood.
  6. Simmer for 5-10 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally.
  7. Once the stew is well-mixed and flavorful, remove from heat and serve with Banku.
