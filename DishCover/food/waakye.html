Waakye

About This Recipe

Waakye is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from rice and beans, cooked together with millet leaves for a unique flavor and color. It is often served with fried plantains, boiled eggs, spaghetti, avocado, shito, and meat or fish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup black-eyed peas or red beans
  • 5-6 dried millet leaves (optional, for color)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda (optional)
  • 5 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Optional Sides:

  • Fried plantains
  • Boiled eggs
  • Spaghetti
  • Avocado slices
  • Fried fish, beef, or chicken
  • Shito (black pepper sauce)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the beans and soak them for a few hours or overnight.
  2. In a pot, add the soaked beans and 4 cups of water.
  3. Add the dried millet leaves (or baking soda) and boil for 30-40 minutes until the beans are tender.
  4. Remove the millet leaves and add the rinsed rice to the pot.
  5. Add salt and additional water if needed.
  6. Stir well, cover the pot, and let the rice cook for 15-20 minutes on low heat.
  7. Serve hot with fried plantains, boiled eggs, spaghetti, avocado, meat, fish, or shito.
