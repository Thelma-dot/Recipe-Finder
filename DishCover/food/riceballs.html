Riceball & Groundnut

About This Recipe

Rice Balls (Omo Tuo) and Groundnut Soup is a popular Ghanaian dish made with soft mashed rice served with a rich, creamy peanut soup. It is commonly enjoyed with meat or fish.

Ingredients for Rice Balls (Omo Tuo)

  • 2 cups rice (preferably soft, short-grain rice)
  • 5 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (optional)

Instructions for Rice Balls (Omo Tuo)

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and place it in a pot with water.
  2. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.
  3. Once the rice becomes soft and sticky, mash it with a wooden spoon.
  4. Shape the mashed rice into smooth balls using wet hands.
  5. Serve hot with groundnut soup.

Ingredients for Groundnut Soup

  • 1 cup peanut butter (groundnut paste)
  • 3 cups water or broth
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (blended or chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (optional, for spice)
  • 1 stock cube (optional)
  • 1 pound meat or fish (chicken, goat, or smoked fish)

Instructions for Groundnut Soup

  1. In a pot, heat a little water and cook the meat or fish with onions, garlic, ginger, salt, and stock cube until tender.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the peanut butter with 1 cup of warm water to form a smooth paste.
  3. Add the peanut paste to the pot with the cooked meat.
  4. Pour in the blended tomatoes and chili powder, then stir well.
  5. Let the soup simmer for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally until the oil rises to the top.
  6. Adjust salt and seasoning to taste.
  7. Serve hot with rice balls.
