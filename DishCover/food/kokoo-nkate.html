Kokoo ne Nkate

About This Recipe

Hausa Koko is a popular Ghanaian spiced millet porridge, often enjoyed as a breakfast dish. It is best served with roasted peanuts (Nkate) or Koose (bean cakes).

Ingredients for Hausa Koko

  • 1 cup millet flour
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cloves (powdered or whole)
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried ginger powder (or fresh ginger, grated)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup sugar (or to taste)

Ingredients for Nkate (Roasted Peanuts)

  • 1 cup raw peanuts (with or without skin)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon water (for roasting)

Instructions for Hausa Koko

  1. In a bowl, mix the millet flour with 1 cup of water to form a smooth paste.
  2. In a pot, bring 3 cups of water to a boil.
  3. Add cloves, ginger, black pepper, and salt to the boiling water.
  4. Slowly pour the millet mixture into the boiling water while stirring continuously.
  5. Keep stirring to prevent lumps and let it cook for about 10–15 minutes until thick and smooth.
  6. Add sugar to taste and serve hot.

Instructions for Roasted Peanuts (Nkate)

  1. Preheat a pan over medium heat.
  2. In a bowl, mix the raw peanuts with a little water and salt.
  3. Spread the peanuts in the pan and roast, stirring occasionally, for about 10–15 minutes until golden brown.
  4. Let the peanuts cool before serving.
  5. Serve the Hausa Koko with roasted peanuts on the side.
