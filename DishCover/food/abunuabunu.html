Abunuabunu

About This Recipe

Abunuabunu is a traditional Ghanaian green soup made from blended kontomire (cocoyam leaves) and served with meat, fish, and snails. It is known for its rich, earthy flavor and is commonly paired with fufu.

Ingredients for Abunuabunu

  • 2 cups kontomire (cocoyam leaves), washed and chopped
  • 1/2 cup turkey berries (Kwahu nsusua) (optional for an authentic taste)
  • 1/2 cup prekese (optional for extra flavor)
  • 1 pound (500g) goat meat, smoked fish, or beef
  • 6–8 large snails, cleaned and washed with lemon or alum
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for spice)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 3 fresh tomatoes
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 seasoning cube (optional)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil

Ingredients for Fufu

  • 2 medium-sized cassavas
  • 2 ripe plantains
  • Water (for boiling)
  • Fufu powder (alternative for instant fufu)

Instructions for Abunuabunu

  1. In a pot, add the goat meat, snails, and smoked fish.
  2. Season with salt, black pepper, ginger, and garlic, and steam for about 10 minutes.
  3. Blend the tomatoes and onions, then add them to the pot.
  4. Add prekese (if using) and pour in water to cover the meat.
  5. Simmer for about 20–30 minutes until the meat and snails are tender.
  6. In a separate pot, boil the kontomire and turkey berries for about 5 minutes.
  7. Blend the kontomire and turkey berries into a smooth green mixture.
  8. Pour the blended green mixture into the soup and stir well.
  9. Allow to simmer for another 10 minutes.
  10. In a separate pan, heat palm oil and pour it into the soup.
  11. Stir well and adjust seasoning as needed.
  12. Remove from heat and serve hot with fufu.

Instructions for Fufu

  1. Peel the cassava and plantains, then cut them into small pieces.
  2. Boil them in a pot of water until soft.
  3. Drain the water and pound them together using a mortar and pestle until smooth and stretchy.
  4. Alternatively, blend the boiled cassava and plantain, then stir over low heat until smooth.
  5. Shape the fufu into small balls and serve with Abunuabunu.
