Fante kenkey with fish & pepper

About This Recipe

Fante Kenkey is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented maize dough, typically served with fried or grilled fish and spicy pepper sauce. It is a staple in Fante communities and enjoyed across Ghana.

Ingredients

For Fante Kenkey:

  • 4 cups maize dough (fermented)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Water (as needed)
  • Banana leaves or corn husks (for wrapping)

For Fried/Grilled Fish:

  • 1 whole tilapia, mackerel, or any preferred fish
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

For Spicy Pepper Sauce (Shito or Fresh Pepper):

  • 4 fresh tomatoes
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (or to taste)
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 teaspoon shrimp powder (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable or palm oil

Instructions

Preparing Fante Kenkey:

  1. In a bowl, mix the maize dough with salt and a little water until smooth.
  2. Divide the dough into two portions.
  3. Cook one portion in a pot over low heat, stirring continuously until thick.
  4. Mix the cooked portion with the uncooked portion until well combined.
  5. Shape the mixture into balls and wrap them in banana leaves or corn husks.
  6. Place the wrapped kenkey in a large pot with water and steam for 3–4 hours.
  7. Once firm, remove from heat and allow it to cool.

Preparing Fried/Grilled Fish:

  1. Clean the fish and make small cuts on the sides.
  2. Rub with salt, black pepper, ginger, garlic, and lemon juice.
  3. Let it marinate for 20–30 minutes.
  4. For frying: Heat oil in a pan and fry the fish until golden brown.
  5. For grilling: Place on a grill and cook, flipping occasionally, until fully cooked.

Preparing Spicy Pepper Sauce:

  1. Blend tomatoes, peppers, and onions into a smooth or slightly chunky paste.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and sauté the mixture for 5–10 minutes.
  3. Add shrimp powder and salt, and let it simmer.
  4. Serve alongside the kenkey and fish.
