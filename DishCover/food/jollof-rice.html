jollof Rice

About This Recipe

Jollof Rice is a popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and a blend of spices. It is a staple in Ghana and is often served with fried plantains, salad, grilled chicken, or fish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1 can (400g) tomato paste
  • 3 fresh tomatoes (blended)
  • 1 red bell pepper (blended)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for spice)
  • 2 cups chicken broth (or water)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable or palm oil
  • 2 bay leaves

Optional Sides:

  • Grilled chicken, beef, or fish
  • Fried plantains
  • Coleslaw or salad
  • Boiled eggs

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Sauté the onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  3. Add the tomato paste, blended tomatoes, and red bell pepper.
  4. Stir in thyme, curry powder, paprika, black pepper, salt, and cayenne pepper.
  5. Cook the sauce for 10-15 minutes until thickened.
  6. Add the rice and mix well to coat with the sauce.
  7. Pour in the chicken broth, add bay leaves, and cover the pot.
  8. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked.
  9. Stir occasionally and add more broth if needed.
  10. Once the rice is soft and fluffy, remove from heat.
  11. Serve hot with grilled meat, fried plantains, salad, or boiled eggs.
