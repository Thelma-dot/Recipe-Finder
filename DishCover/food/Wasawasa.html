Wasawasa

About This Recipe

Zowey is a popular Ghanaian dish made from ground millet or corn, often enjoyed as a snack or light meal. It is typically served with a variety of sauces or soups, giving it a rich, savory flavor. Zowey is known for its unique texture and flavor, which is both satisfying and filling.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups millet or cornmeal (ground)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (optional, for greasing)
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper (optional, for spice)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix the ground millet or cornmeal with a little water to create a smooth paste.
  2. In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil, and add salt to the water.
  3. Slowly add the millet or cornmeal paste into the boiling water, stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming. Keep stirring until the mixture thickens and begins to pull away from the sides of the pot, about 10-15 minutes.
  4. Once the Zowey has thickened and formed a smooth consistency, remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  5. Grease your hands with a little vegetable oil, then shape the Zowey into small balls or servings, depending on your preference.
  6. Serve Zowey with a savory tomato sauce, stew, or any soup of your choice, or enjoy it on its own as a satisfying snack or meal.
