Mpotompoto

About This Recipe

Mpotompoto, also known as yam porridge, is a thick and hearty Ghanaian dish made with yam, vegetables, and spices. It is nutritious and often enjoyed as a comforting meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium yam (peeled and cut into cubes)
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (blended or chopped)
  • 1 bell pepper (chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger and garlic paste
  • 1 cup water or broth
  • 1/2 cup smoked fish or meat (optional)
  • 1 handful spinach or kontomire (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat the palm oil in a pot and sauté the chopped onions until fragrant.
  2. Add the blended or chopped tomatoes, bell pepper, and ginger-garlic paste. Cook for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Stir in the ground crayfish, salt, and ground pepper.
  4. Add the yam cubes and pour in the water or broth. Stir well.
  5. Cover and cook on medium heat for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Once the yam is soft, mash some of the pieces to thicken the porridge.
  7. Add smoked fish or meat (if using) and stir well.
  8. For extra nutrition, add spinach or kontomire and cook for another 2 minutes.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy!
