Akple with Abobi

About This Recipe

Akple is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from cornmeal, often served with soups or stews. It pairs perfectly with Abobi Tadi, a flavorful anchovy stew.

Ingredients for Akple

  • 2 cups cornmeal
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions for Akple

  1. Boil 3 cups of water in a pot.
  2. Mix 1 cup of water with the cornmeal in a separate bowl to form a smooth paste.
  3. Slowly add the cornmeal paste to the boiling water, stirring continuously.
  4. Keep stirring while cooking for 10-15 minutes until it thickens.
  5. Once smooth and firm, shape into balls and set aside.

Ingredients for Abobi Tadi (Anchovy Stew)

  • 2 cups dried anchovies (abobi)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (blended)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable or palm oil

Instructions for Abobi Tadi (Anchovy Stew)

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions, garlic, and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and blended tomatoes, then cook for 10 minutes until thickened.
  4. Season with paprika, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper.
  5. Add the dried anchovies and stir well.
  6. Let the stew simmer for another 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Serve hot with Akple.
