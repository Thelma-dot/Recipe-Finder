Ampesi & Kontomire

About This Recipe

Ampesi is a traditional Ghanaian dish made with boiled yam, plantain, cocoyam, or cassava. It is often paired with Kontomire Stew, a rich and flavorful sauce made from cocoyam leaves (kontomire), tomatoes, and palm oil.

Ingredients for Ampesi

  • 2 medium-sized yams (or substitute with plantains, cocoyam, or cassava)
  • Water (for boiling)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (optional)

Ingredients for Kontomire Stew

  • 2 cups kontomire (cocoyam leaves), washed and chopped
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger (grated)
  • 3 fresh tomatoes (blended or chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 smoked fish or dry fish (deboned and flaked)
  • 1 cup precooked or canned mackerel (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for extra spice)
  • 1 seasoning cube (optional)
  • 1/2 cup ground melon seeds (agushie, optional for thickness)
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (optional, for garnish)

Instructions for Ampesi

  1. Peel and cut the yam (or plantain, cassava, or cocoyam) into medium-sized pieces.
  2. Rinse and place them in a pot, then add enough water to cover.
  3. Boil for 15–20 minutes until soft.
  4. Drain and set aside, ready to be served with the stew.

Instructions for Kontomire Stew

  1. Heat the palm oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions, garlic, and ginger, then sauté until fragrant.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and cook for about 5 minutes.
  4. Add blended or chopped tomatoes and cook until the sauce thickens.
  5. Season with salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, and seasoning cube if using.
  6. Add the smoked fish, stirring well to combine.
  7. Stir in the chopped kontomire leaves and let it cook for about 10 minutes.
  8. If using agushie, blend it with a little water and add it to the stew, letting it cook until it solidifies.
  9. Add the mackerel (if using) and allow the stew to simmer for another 5 minutes.
  10. Garnish with boiled eggs and serve hot with ampesi.
