Akyeke

About This Recipe

Akyeke is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented cassava that is grated, dried, and processed into small pellets or granules. It is typically served as a side dish with fish, stew, or soup. Akyeke has a soft, slightly sour taste and is often enjoyed with a variety of flavorful sauces.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Akyeke (fermented cassava flakes)
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 small onion, chopped (optional, for flavor)
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable broth or meat stock (optional, for richer flavor)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the Akyeke in cold water to remove any excess starch or dirt.
  2. In a pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil. Add a pinch of salt and stir in the Akyeke.
  3. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the Akyeke cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  4. If you want to enhance the flavor, add vegetable oil or meat stock to the Akyeke during cooking. Stir to combine well and cook for an additional 5 minutes, until the Akyeke has absorbed the liquid and become soft and fluffy.
  5. If you like, you can add chopped onions or other vegetables to the Akyeke for extra flavor.
  6. Once the Akyeke is fully cooked, serve it hot as a side dish with your favorite stew, fish, or soup.
