Pito

About This Recipe

Pito is a traditional Ghanaian fermented drink made from millet or sorghum. It is slightly alcoholic, naturally fermented, and commonly enjoyed in the northern regions of Ghana.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups millet or sorghum
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon yeast (optional, for faster fermentation)

Instructions

  1. Rinse the millet or sorghum thoroughly and soak it in water overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked grains and grind them into a smooth paste using a blender or grinding mill.
  3. Add 4 cups of water to the paste and let it ferment for 2-3 days in a covered container at room temperature.
  4. After fermentation, boil the mixture in a large pot with the remaining 4 cups of water for about an hour, stirring occasionally.
  5. Once thickened, remove from heat and allow it to cool.
  6. Strain the mixture using a fine sieve or cheesecloth to separate the liquid from the solids.
  7. Let the strained liquid sit for a few more hours to develop its flavor.
  8. Serve slightly chilled or at room temperature.
