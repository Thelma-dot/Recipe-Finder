Zomkom

About This Recipe

Zomkom is a traditional Ghanaian dish made from fermented corn dough, commonly eaten as a snack or a side dish. It is served with a variety of sauces, stews, or soups. Its soft, slightly tangy flavor pairs well with a spicy, flavorful sauce or soup.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fermented corn dough (or cornmeal if fermented dough is not available)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (optional, for greasing)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix the fermented corn dough with a little water to make a smooth paste. Add more water to adjust the consistency if needed. The dough should be thick but smooth.
  2. Bring a pot of water to a boil, and add a pinch of salt to the water.
  3. Using your hands or a spoon, shape the corn dough into small balls or small portions. You can grease your hands with a little oil to prevent the dough from sticking to them.
  4. Carefully drop the dough balls into the boiling water and cook them for 15-20 minutes or until they float to the top and are fully cooked through.
  5. Once cooked, remove the Zomkom from the water and drain any excess water.
  6. Serve your Zomkom with a spicy tomato sauce, stew, or soup of your choice. It can also be enjoyed on its own as a light snack or appetizer.
